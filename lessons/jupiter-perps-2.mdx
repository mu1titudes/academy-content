## Trading on Jupiter Perps: Your First Position

Opening a futures position on a traditional broker usually involves margin agreements, account tiers, and slow order routing. On **Jupiter Perps**, you just connect your wallet, pick a direction, and trade - with every fee and liquidation number visible before you click. This lesson is the hands-on follow-up to [Understanding Jupiter Perps](/lessons/jupiter-perps-1). It walks you through opening your first position, handling limit orders, managing the trade, and troubleshooting common issues.

### The Interface

At **[Jupiter Perps](https://jup.ag/perps)** the layout has three main areas: the chart on the left, the trading form on the right, and your open positions below. The form shows every number that matters - entry price, liquidation price, total fees - before you confirm.

![Chart, trading form, and open positions at a glance](https://static.academy.jup.ag/images/illustrations/jupiter-perps-app.png)

### Opening Your First Position

Here's a step-by-step walkthrough. To keep risk low while you learn, this example uses 2x leverage.

1. Go to **jup.ag/perps** and click **Connect**. Select your Solana wallet (Jupiter Mobile, Jupiter Extension, Phantom, Solflare, or Backpack).
2. In the market selector, pick **SOL-PERP**, **ETH-PERP**, or **wBTC-PERP**.
3. Choose **Long** (expecting price to rise) or **Short** (expecting it to fall).
4. In the **You're Paying** section, pick your collateral token and enter the amount. Jupiter auto-swaps if needed.
5. Adjust the **Leverage slider**. Start with 2x. Position size = collateral × leverage.
6. Pick **Market** (fills now) or **Limit** (fills at a target price - see next section).
7. Review the **Order Summary**: entry price, liquidation price, fees, borrow rate.
8. Click **LONG** or **SHORT** and approve in your wallet. Your position appears in the Positions tab below the chart.

### How Limit Orders Work: The Keeper Model

Market orders fill immediately. Limit orders need to wait until price reaches your target. Jupiter handles this with a **keeper model**: an automated bot watches oracle prices on your behalf.

![The four-step lifecycle of a limit order](https://static.academy.jup.ag/images/illustrations/img6_keeper.png)

### Managing an Open Position

Once a position is open, you have several tools in the Positions tab:

* **Take Profit.** Automatically closes at a target above entry (for longs) to lock gains.
* **Stop Loss.** Automatically closes at a level below entry (for longs) to cap losses.
* **Edit collateral.** Add more to push liquidation further from market, or withdraw to increase leverage.
* **Partial close.** Close a portion while keeping the rest open - useful for scaling out of a winner.

Your liquidation price isn't static. It drifts over time because borrow fees reduce effective collateral. Check it at least daily for leverage above 5x.

### Three Real Use Cases

**1. Leveraged long on momentum**

SOL is trending up. You deposit 10 SOL at $150 and open a **5x long** (50 SOL position, ~$7,500). Take Profit at $165 (+10%), Stop Loss at $143 (-5%). If SOL hits $165, you earn ~50% on collateral. If $143, you lose ~25%.

**2. Hedging an existing holding**

You hold SOL long-term but worry about a short-term drop. Selling would interrupt your long-term thesis. Instead, you open a small **short** sized to part of your holdings. If SOL drops, the short profits offset your portfolio loss. Cost: borrow fees while the hedge is active.

**3. Short-term scalping**

You want to trade intraday volatility without tying up much capital. **2-3x leverage** keeps liquidation far away; the 0.06% base fee is the main cost; borrow fees are negligible over minutes. This is the lowest-risk way to use perps.

### Troubleshooting: Common Issues

**"My position opened at a different price than the chart showed."** The price between request and execution may be different, especially during volatile periods where the oracle price can change quickly and dramatically.

**"I was liquidated but the price never hit my liquidation level."** Your liquidation price moved because of accumulated borrow fees. Fees erode collateral, which shifts the liquidation price over time.

**"My limit order didn't execute even though price hit my target."** The keeper only executes when oracle conditions are clean. If the feed was stale or slippage tolerance was too tight, your collateral is returned instead of a bad fill.

**"Insufficient liquidity error."** Pool utilization for that asset is near 100%. Try a smaller position size, or wait for utilization to drop.

**"Borrow fees are higher than expected."** You're probably above target utilization. The dual slope model kicks in aggressively above target. Check the current rate before opening new long-duration positions.
