## Start Predicting Everything with Jupiter Predict

Have you ever wanted to trade on presidential elections, or if aliens do exist? In the days before the internet, you would have to find a person who is not only just as interested in the subject as you, but also has a contrary view to yours and is willing to take you up on it. Only then would you have a deal.

With Jupiter Predict, you can now do this with people from all over the world. By leveraging PolyMarket's infrastructure, you can trade on anything, starting from politics and ending with the weather, so long as there's a market for it.

It works similarly to traditional sports predictions - albeit you can do that on Jupiter Predict as well. The main difference is that while the prices for sport matches are set by a single operator, for onchain prediction markets the prices are set by the users themselves, through supply and demand.

### How Does It Work?

In essence, the markets on predict are orderbooks with orders set at different prices by traders. There are two sides for each market: "YES" and "NO". If you want to place a trade, you need to buy contracts of the outcome you believe will be the winning one. The prices of the contracts range from 0 to 100 cents, and are defined by trading activity and human sentiments, rather than a single entity.

What is novel for Jupiter Predict is that price itself serves a function. The closer it is to 0 cents, the less likely the event is to happen and vice versa. So, this reflects the basic principle of high-risk high-reward.

It is important to note that price is decided by humans, and humans make a lot of mistakes. Often the public may not see the whole picture, or just buy into the more popular vote, which might lead to overconfident odds. That's why information asymmetries arise, making it possible to earn outsized gains if you have specific knowledge about the market.

### Prediction Example

Let's say you believe in Solana and, using $100, want to predict that SOL reaches $240 this year. The price of a "YES" contract is set at 10 cents. Therefore, with your capital you can buy 1,000 contracts.

In the case where the SOL token does reach the $240 mark this year, the market will resolve to "YES" and your contracts will increase in price to 100 cents each, raising the value of your stack to $1,000. This would result in $900 of profit minus the platform fees.

If in the unfortunate event where the SOL token does not reach the $240 mark this year, the market will eventually resolve to "NO", and your contracts will drop to 0 cents each, resulting in a loss of $100.

Notably, the prediction tokens are tradeable and will fluctuate in price based on supply and demand. If the price of SOL goes to $200, more people will be buying into this market, which will increase demand and naturally increase the price of your prediction tokens. Then you can choose to sell them for profit without waiting for the final settlement.

### The Importance of Liquidity

It's important to note that each market must have sufficient liquidity for you to place a trade. In prediction markets, the liquidity depends on the limit orders of traders, so when you want to trade, you can market buy from ask side, or set limit order yourself and be on the bid side.

* **Bid** - the highest price a buyer is willing to pay for an asset
* **Ask** - the lowest price a seller is willing to accept for an asset

This is to say that for any trade you want to make, there has to be a person on the other side who will take the opposite stance. If you scream "ALIENS DO NOT EXIST" and want to trade $1M into 80 cents per contract, another user must scream back "THEY DO EXIST" while backing their words with $250k at 20 cents per contract.

### Risks of Trading on Predict

Prediction markets definitely pose risks. You can lose money predicting on the wrong outcome or chip away at your portfolio by overtrading. The regular rules of trading apply to prediction markets as well.

One risk specific to prediction markets is the rule set. Each market has a set of written rules that define how and when it resolves. These rules can be easy to understand (i.e. a sports team wins a match), or it can be extremely nuanced, like in politics or economics. A "YES" contract in those markets may have specific conditions about what counts as a win or loss. Always read the resolution rules before placing a trade. If the rules are unclear to you, that uncertainty is part of the risk.
