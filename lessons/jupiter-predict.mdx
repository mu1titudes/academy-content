## Start Predicting Everything with Jupiter Predict

Have you ever wanted to trade on presidential elections, or if aliens do exist? In the days before the internet, you would have to find a person who is not only just as interested in the subject as you, but also has a contrary view to yours and is willing to take you up on it. Only then would you have a deal.

With Jupiter Predict, you can now do this with people from all over the world. By leveraging Kalshi and Polymarket infrastructure, you can trade on anything, starting from politics and ending with the weather, so long as there's a market for it.

It works similarly to traditional sports predictions - albeit you can do that on Jupiter Predict as well. The main difference is that while the prices for sport matches are set by a single operator, for onchain prediction markets the prices are set by traders across these venues, through supply and demand.

### How Does It Work?

In essence, the markets on predict are orderbooks with orders set at different prices by traders. There are two sides for each market: "YES" and "NO". If you want to place a trade, you need to buy contracts of the outcome you believe will be the winning one. The prices of the contracts range from $0.01 to $0.99 USDC, and are defined by trading activity and human sentiments, rather than a single entity.

What is novel for Jupiter Predict is that price itself serves a function. The closer it is to 0 cents, the less likely the event is to happen and vice versa. So, this reflects the basic principle of high-risk high-reward.

It is important to note that price is decided by humans, and humans make a lot of mistakes. Often the public may not see the whole picture, or just buy into the more popular vote, which might lead to overconfident odds. That's why information asymmetries arise, making it possible to earn outsized gains if you have specific knowledge about the market.

### Degen Mode: Not for the Faint-Hearted

Beyond the standard event markets, Jupiter Predict also offers a Degen Mode, a section dedicated to short-duration micro-bets on crypto price movements. Instead of waiting weeks or months for an election or a championship to resolve, you predict whether a token's price will go Up or Down over a 5-minute or 15-minute window. Available tokens currently include SOL, BTC, ETH, XRP, BNB, DOGE, and Hyperliquid. The contract logic is identical to the rest of Predict; it's just a faster, higher-frequency way to express a short-term view on the market.

### Prediction Example

Let's say you believe in Solana and, using $100 USDC, want to predict that SOL reaches $240 this year. The price of a "YES" contract is set at $0.10 USDC. Therefore, with your capital you can buy 1,000 contracts.

In the case where the SOL token does reach the $240 mark this year, the market will resolve to "YES" and each of your contracts will pay out $1 USDC at settlement, raising the value of your stack to $1,000. This would result in $900 of profit minus the platform fees. This can be claimed from your Profile page a short while after that market closes.

If in the unfortunate event where the SOL token does not reach the $240 mark this year, the market will eventually resolve to "NO", and your contracts will expire worthless, resulting in a loss of $100.

Notably, the prediction tokens are tradeable and will fluctuate in price based on supply and demand. If the price of SOL goes to $200, more people will be buying into this market, which will increase demand and naturally increase the price of your prediction tokens. Then you can choose to sell them for profit without waiting for the final settlement.

### The Importance of Liquidity

It's important to note that each market must have sufficient liquidity for you to place a trade. In prediction markets, the liquidity depends on the limit orders of traders, so when you want to trade, you can market buy from the ask side, or set a limit order yourself and be on the bid side.

* **Bid** - the highest price a buyer is willing to pay for an asset
* **Ask** - the lowest price a seller is willing to accept for an asset

This is to say that for any trade you want to make, there has to be a person on the other side who will take the opposite stance. If you scream "ALIENS DO NOT EXIST" and want to trade $1M into 80 cents per contract, another user must scream back "THEY DO EXIST" while backing their words with $250k at 20 cents per contract.

### Risks of Trading on Predict

Prediction markets definitely pose risks. You can lose money predicting on the wrong outcome or chip away at your portfolio by overtrading. The regular rules of trading apply to prediction markets as well.

One risk specific to prediction markets is the rule set. Each market has a set of written rules that define how and when it resolves. These rules can be easy to understand (i.e., a sports team wins a match), or it can be extremely nuanced, like in politics or economics. A "YES" contract in those markets may have specific conditions about what counts as a win or loss. Always read the resolution rules before placing a trade. If the rules are unclear to you, that uncertainty is part of the risk.

Note that Jupiter Predict is not available to users in the US or South Korea.

### Understanding the Fees

Every executed trade on Jupiter Predict carries two fees: one charged by the upstream venue (Kalshi or Polymarket), and a matching fee charged by Jupiter. Fees scale with contract price and trade size, with trades near $0.50 carrying the highest fees and those near $0.01 or $0.99 the lowest. Unfilled orders and payout claims are free.

### Place Your Trade on Jupiter Predict

**Step 1**

Go to [Jupiter Predict](https://jup.ag/prediction) and connect your Solana wallet. Trades and payouts are settled in USDC — the Jupiter Aggregator can convert another asset into USDC at entry.

**Step 2**

Find a market that you are interested in through the categories section or type it out in the search bar.

**Step 3**

Check the rules for the market resolution terms. Buy a number of contracts with "Yes" or "No" options. You can market buy at ask prices or try to get filled lower with limit orders.

**Step 4**

Now that you have successfully acquired contracts, continue observing the market. You can sell at any time if you want to get out of the position, or wait for the resolution of the market.
