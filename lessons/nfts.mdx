## Owning NFTs on Solana

In traditional finance, a deed proves you own a house, a stock certificate proves you own shares of a company, and a numbered art print proves you own a specific copy of a work. Each is a verifiable record of ownership, often issued by a trusted authority. **NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens)** are the digital equivalent on a blockchain: unique tokens that prove ownership of a specific item, recorded publicly and transparently. This lesson explains what NFTs are on Solana, why they matter, how to receive and manage them with **Jupiter Wallet**, and how to stay safe.

### What Makes an NFT Different

A regular token like SOL or USDC is **fungible**, meaning every unit is identical and interchangeable. One USDC is exactly equal to any other USDC. Fungible tokens are also **divisible**, you can split 1 SOL into 0.1 SOL and 0.9 SOL, and the two parts together are still worth exactly 1 SOL. 

An **NFT is non-fungible**: each one is unique, and it cannot be split. You can't send "10% of an NFT", you either own it or you don't. Two NFTs from the same collection share style and origin, but each has its own ID, ownership history, and metadata.

![Fungible tokens are interchangeable. NFTs are each one of a kind](TBD)

What you actually own when you "own an NFT" is a record on the blockchain that points to the asset (an image, video, or file hosted on decentralized storage). That record proves you, and only you, control it from your wallet.

### Real Use Cases

NFTs are more than just profile pictures. The most common categories:

* **Profile pictures (PFPs) and art.** Collections like Mad Lads, Claynosaurz, and SMB. Holders often get community access and airdrops.
* **Gaming.** In-game items, characters, and land plots.
* **Tickets and access.** Event tickets, membership passes, gated content.
* **Domain names.** Solana Name Service domains (yourname.sol) are NFTs that you own, which replaces your public address with a human-readable name.
* **Position NFTs.** A growing DeFi pattern where your position in a protocol is itself an NFT. [Jupiter Lend](lessons/jupiter-earn) (like jlWSOL or jlUSDC) are position NFTs that act like reciepts. And because these are transferable, these positions can interact with other DeFi protocols.

> **Critical: Never burn your Position NFT**
>
> If you use Jupiter Lend, the Position NFT is required to manage and withdraw your funds. Burning it destroys your access to the underlying assets.

### How Jupiter Wallet Handles NFTs

**Jupiter Wallet** (mobile and browser extension) is a non-custodial Solana wallet built around trading. Only you control your keys.

![Jupiter Wallet stores your NFTs. Marketplaces and explorers display them](TBD)

**What it does:**

* Receives any NFT to your Solana address (same address as for tokens).
* **Displays your NFT collection visually.** Open the **NFTs** tab in Jupiter Mobile to browse your holdings as a grid with images and names.
* Holds your NFTs securely until you transfer them.
* Connects to marketplaces like Magic Eden and Tensor for buy / sell / list.
* Shows every transaction for review before you sign.

It's important to note that Jupiter Wallet does not run its own NFT marketplace. Buying, selling, and listing happen on third-party venues like Magic Eden or Tensor.

### Risks and How to Stay Safe

> **Careful: NFT scams are pervasive and creative**
>
> Most lost NFTs are not stolen by hackers. They are signed away by holders who didn't read the transaction. Treat every signature request as suspicious until proven safe.

![The four most common NFT scams and how to avoid each](TBD)

**Mitigation checklist:**

* **Read every transaction before signing.** If a popup mentions transferring NFTs you didn't intend to move, reject it.
* **Use the wallet's transaction scanner.** Jupiter Wallet flags suspicious transactions. Take warnings seriously.
* **Never type your seed phrase.** Not on a website, not in a DM, not in a "support" form. These might be [phishing attempts](/lessons/safety-phishing). Real support never asks for it.
* **Ignore unsolicited NFTs.** If a random NFT appears, don't click any link inside it.
* **Bookmark official marketplaces.** Type magiceden.io or tensor.trade directly, never click ads or DM links.
* **Use a hardware wallet** for valuable holdings. Jupiter Wallet supports Ledger, Keystone, and Trezor.

### Tutorial: Receiving and Verifying Your First NFT

Whether someone is sending you an NFT or you just minted one, the flow is the same.

1. Open **Jupiter Wallet** (mobile or extension) and unlock it.
2. Tap **Receive** and copy your Solana address (or share the QR code).
3. Send the address to whoever is transferring the NFT, or paste it into the mint site. The transfer executes on-chain in about 0.4 seconds.
4. To verify it, open the **NFTs** tab inside Jupiter Mobile, the new NFT will appear in your gallery with its image and name. You can also paste your address into **solscan.io** or connect to **magiceden.io** / **tensor.trade** for more detail.
5. Bookmark the marketplaces you use. Never connect through links sent by strangers.
