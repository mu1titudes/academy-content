## Tokenomics: Assessing the Legitimacy of Your Tokens

Tokenomics (short for *Token Economics*) is the economic design of a crypto token, factoring in supply, distribution, emissions and utility. Properly executed tokenomics can help to support a token's long-term growth.

In traditional finance, central banks control the money supply, interest rates and inflation through quantitative easing or tightening. Crypto tokenomics can act in a similar way, but allow better transparency due to clear visibility of the information on the blockchain.

Tokenomics is a broad concept that comprises many factors. In combination, those factors can help users understand the design of a crypto token, to better inform their investment decisions.

### Token Supply

The token supply is the number of tokens issued on the blockchain. However, not all tokens outlined in the tokenomics can be immediately available. So, these terms are important to understand supply mechanics:

- **Max Supply**: the maximum number of tokens that can ever exist according to the protocol's rules.
- **Total Supply**: the number of tokens that currently exist onchain. This is max supply minus any tokens that have been burned. It changes over time.
- **Circulating Supply**: the portion of total supply that's actively in the market - total supply minus tokens that are locked, vested, or held in the treasury.

In the case of Bitcoin, the current total supply is around 20 million tokens, while the max supply is capped at 21 million BTC (as of early 2026).

### Market Value

- **Market Capitalization (MC)**. The total value of the tokens available in the market, usually represented in USD values. It is calculated by multiplying circulating supply by price of the token.

- **Fully Diluted Valuation (FDV)**. The max supply multiplied by current token price. FDV represents the theoretical market cap if every token that could ever exist were already circulating at the current price.

### Token Distribution

When the token gets launched, it is distributed to different parties. They often include:

- Community allocation - received by users of the token project, distributed as rewards for loyalty
- Team allocation - received by core team and often locked with a vesting schedule
- Investor allocation - portion reserved to initial investors of the project
- Future grants and rewards - portion subject to a vesting schedule, reserved for future incentive campaigns

It is crucial to understand how tokens are distributed. If the majority of the tokens are locked or handled by a minority of the holders, it can lead to price manipulation.

### Emissions and Burns

The total supply of the token can be increased by emissions if the *mint authority* is not disabled. This means that new tokens can be created on the blockchain, increasing the supply and causing inflation.

This can be done according to the design of a token and its utility. However, bad actors can also use the [mint authority](/lessons/safety-rugs) to create an infinite number of new tokens and sell them into the market.

Burning destroys tokens and removes them from circulation. It is a countermeasure for emissions that token projects can use to control supply and inflation.

### Token Utility

Tokens are created for usage. The utility defines tokenomics, so often projects may have more than one token dedicated for a certain utility. These are some of the token types and utilities that exist nowadays:

- Gas tokens - native network tokens used for transacting and security (BTC, SOL, ETH)
- [Governance tokens](/lessons/jupiter-governance) - used for participating in onchain voting (JUP, MET, CLOUD)
- Stablecoins - tokens pegged in value to real-world assets (JupUSD, USDC)
- Gaming tokens - used as in-game currency, rewards, or assets in Web3 games

[Governance tokens](/lessons/jupiter-governance) often represent ownership; that's why their supply is immutable, so that the shareholder value never gets diluted. Meanwhile, gaming tokens require emissions to reward the player base. As for stablecoins, authorized emissions are healthy because the newly issued tokens are backed by real-world assets.

### Importance of Evaluating Tokenomics

Understanding tokenomics can help to evaluate the legitimacy of the token in the long run. The visibility of data on the blockchain makes it possible for retail market participants to make investing decisions.
