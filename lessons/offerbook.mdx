## Setting Your Own Terms with Jupiter Offerbook

In traditional finance, a private credit agreement between two parties -  a pawnshop loan or a bilateral business loan - works by fixing every condition upfront. Both sides agree on the collateral, the rate, and the duration before anything is signed. Once the terms are set, nothing changes until maturity. There are no surprise margin calls, no mid-loan adjustments, and no ambiguity about the outcome.

Jupiter Offerbook brings this model on-chain. It is a permissionless, peer-to-peer money market where borrowers and lenders set their own terms, match with each other directly, and operate within a fully fixed loan structure from start to finish.

### What Is Jupiter Offerbook?

Offerbook is a peer-to-peer credit market built on Solana, where users borrow and lend USDC against on-chain assets at fixed rates, for a fixed duration, with no price-based liquidations.

Unlike traditional money markets - including [Jupiter Borrow](/lessons/jupiter-borrow) - Offerbook does not rely on real-time market pricing or continuous collateral monitoring. Risk is not managed by tracking the price of your collateral in real time. Instead, it is managed by time: every loan has a known duration, a known rate, and a known outcome at maturity. Both sides of the trade define their terms, publish an offer, and wait for a match. Once matched, nothing changes until the loan ends.

### Key Features

#### Fixed-Term Loans & Key Terms

Every loan on Offerbook is built around five parameters. Understanding how they interact is the foundation for building any strategy on the platform.

- **Collateral:** The on-chain asset locked by the borrower for the duration of the loan. Any verified token on <Term id="solana">Solana</Term> can serve as collateral. If the loan is not repaid by maturity, the lender becomes eligible to claim the collateral - but the borrower can still repay until the lender claims it.
- **LTV (Loan-to-Value):** The ratio between the borrowed USDC amount and the value of the collateral. For example, locking $1,000 of <Term id="sol">SOL</Term> and borrowing 700 USDC sets an <Term id="ltv">LTV</Term> of 70%. The borrowed amount and <Term id="ltv">LTV</Term> are linked - adjusting one automatically updates the other.
- **APR / APY:** The <Term id="apr">APR</Term> is the annualized cost of borrowing, set by the borrower or by the lender when creating an offer. The <Term id="apy">APY</Term> is the annualized return for the lender, or the annualized cost for the borrower. Both are fully defined by the user, market-driven, and fixed for the entire duration of the loan.
- **Loan Duration:** The fixed time period, in days, during which the collateral is locked and the loan is active. Once a loan starts, the duration cannot be changed. The loan must be repaid before maturity, at maturity, the loan becomes claimable by the lender. The borrower can still repay after maturity as long as the lender has not yet claimed the collateral. Once the lender claims, the collateral is transferred in full, regardless of its current market value.
- **Loan expiration:** Represents the time period during which a loan offer is available to be filled. If a loan reaches its expiration date without being filled, the offer is invalidated and the offer creator must renew or cancel it.


Higher LTVs generally come with higher interest rates and are more commonly used for shorter durations. Lower LTVs carry less risk for lenders and tend to pair with longer loan periods.

#### The Loan Lifecycle

A loan on Offerbook follows a simple, linear path with no moving parts once it begins.

1. **Create an offer.** Either a borrower or a lender can publish an offer with their desired terms. All offers have an expiration date, currently fixed at 1 day for lending offers and up to 7 days for borrowing offers. Since Offerbook does not rely on a price oracle, this expiration mechanism is necessary to allow offers to adapt to changing market conditions and collateral price fluctuations.


2. **Get matched.** Another user accepts the offer, either in full or partially. Partial acceptance is available for all offers except those using <Term id="nft">NFT</Term> collateral, since an <Term id="nft">NFT</Term> cannot be partially transferred. Each loan offer can be shared directly via a link. You can send it to someone to match your terms or share it on social media.

3. **Loan runs.** The moment an offer is accepted, the loan starts. <Term id="collateral">Collateral</Term> is locked in a smart contract, USDC is transferred, and all terms are fixed and immutable. No margin calls, no price monitoring, no changes of any kind can occur during the loan.

4. **Repay or collateral claim.** The borrower can repay at any point to reclaim their collateral in full. After maturity, the lender becomes eligible to manually claim the collateral - but the borrower can still repay until the lender does so. Once the lender claims, the transfer is final.

#### Fees, Explained Simply

Offerbook charges a **25% fee on the interest** paid by borrowers when a loan is filled. A 10% fee on the interest earned by lenders is charged when the loan is repaid.This fee is taken from the interest amount, not the principal. A 0.1% fee is charged on the collateral transfer in the event of a default.

At each of these moments, if a platform fee is charged, that fee can be split into referral rewards.
By default, the fee is divided like this:
- 20% goes to the referred user
- 30% goes to the referrer side
- 50% stays with the protocol


### A Real Example

A user holds a tokenized asset valued at approximately $10,000. The asset has limited onchain liquidity, making it difficult to sell quickly without significant price impact. Rather than selling, the user wants to access USDC for 3 days to deploy in another strategy.

**They create an Offerbook offer with the following terms:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| <Term id="collateral">Collateral</Term> | Tokenized assets (~$10,000) |
| Borrowed asset | 8,000 USDC |
| <Term id="ltv">LTV</Term> | 80% |
| Loan duration | 3 days |
| Fixed <Term id="apr">APR</Term> | 35% |

A lender reviews the offer and accepts it in full. The loan starts immediately - collateral is locked, 8,000 USDC is transferred to the borrower.

**At maturity, the borrower repays:**

| Detail | Amount |
|---|---|
| Interest owed (35% <Term id="apr">APR</Term> × 3 days) | ~$23.01 |
| <Term id="protocol">Protocol</Term> fee charge to the borrower (25% of interest) | ~$5.75 |
| <Term id="protocol">Protocol</Term> fee charge to the lender (10% of the interest) | ~$2.30 |
| Interest received by lender | ~$20.709 |
| <Term id="collateral">Collateral</Term> returned | Full tokenized assets |

Throughout the entire three days, no price-based liquidation could occur - regardless of how the RWA's market price moved. The borrower used their liquidity freely, and the lender earned a known, fixed return.

**If the borrower had not repaid before maturity:** the lender becomes eligible to claim the collateral. The borrower can still repay after maturity, as long as the lender has not yet claimed. Once claimed, the entire collateral is transferred to the lender with a 0.1% fee deducted at the point of transfer.

### Risks & What to Keep in Mind

Offerbook removes certain risks that exist in traditional money markets - but it does not remove all risk. Understanding the trade-off is essential before using the platform.

**For borrowers:** 
* Because there are no margin calls or liquidations during the loan, it is easy to lose track of when repayment is due. After maturity, the lender can claim the collateral at any time. The borrower can still repay after maturity as long as the lender has not yet claimed - but there are no reminders or guarantees the lender will wait. Repayment timing is entirely the borrower's responsibility.
* Additionally, the full interest for the agreed duration is owed regardless of when early repayment occurs - there is no pro-rated discount for repaying ahead of schedule.

**For lenders:** 
* <Term id="collateral">Collateral</Term> value is not monitored during the loan. If the collateral asset loses significant value between the start of the loan and maturity, the lender may receive an asset worth less than the USDC they provided. The higher the <Term id="ltv">LTV</Term> and the longer the duration, the greater this exposure.
* When evaluating an offer, lenders should consider the liquidity of the collateral, its volatility over the loan period, and how comfortable they would be holding that asset outright at maturity.

## Let's Use Offerbook

### As a Borrower

**Step 1: Create a Borrow Offer or Accept an Existing One**

Navigate to the official Jupiter Offerbook website. Browse existing offers and accept one directly, or select "Borrow" to publish your own offer. Choose your collateral asset and amount, set your desired USDC amount (or <Term id="ltv">LTV</Term> - they update together), loan duration, expiration, and <Term id="apr">APR</Term>. Review the estimated interest and fees, then publish your offer.

**Step 2: Wait for a Match**

Your offer is live in the offerbook for up to 24 hours. Once another user accepts it - in full or partially - the loan starts immediately. You will receive USDC and your collateral will be locked.

**Step 3: Repay Before Maturity**

Monitor your loan's maturity date from your dashboard. Repay at any time before maturity to reclaim your collateral in full. Remember: the full interest for the agreed duration is owed regardless of when you repay.

---

### As a Lender

**Step 1: Create a Lend Offer or Accept an Existing One**

Navigate to the official Jupiter Offerbook website. Browse existing borrow offers and accept one directly, or select "Lend" to publish your own offer with your preferred collateral, USDC amount, duration, and <Term id="apy">APY</Term>. Evaluate each offer based on collateral quality, LTV, and loan duration.

Thanks to the escrow wallet, you can set up multiple lending offers using the same USDC as collateral.
This allows you to reuse your available balance efficiently without needing to deposit funds multiple times.

It enables more flexible and capital-efficient lending strategies. Once an offer is accepted, it will use the funds, while the others will remain paused until USDC is available again in your wallet.

**Step 2: Wait for a Match**

If you have published a lend offer, it remains live for up to 24 hours. Once a borrower accepts it, the loan starts and your USDC is transferred. <Term id="collateral">Collateral</Term> is locked in a smart contract for the duration of the loan.

**Step 3: Collect at Maturity**

If the borrower repays, you receive your USDC plus interest (minus the protocol fee). If they do not repay before maturity, you can manually claim the collateral. The borrower can still repay after maturity until you claim it.
